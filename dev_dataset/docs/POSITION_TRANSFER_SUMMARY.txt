WHAT ARE POSITION TRANSFERS?
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Position transfers are IN-KIND CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS where you transfer
securities (stocks, bonds, ETFs, Treasury bills) into your LLC instead of cash.

This is DIFFERENT from:
  • Cash contributions (MoneyLink, wire, check)
  • Buying securities INSIDE the LLC with LLC funds

Position transfers occur when you:
  1. Own securities in your personal brokerage account
  2. Transfer those positions to your LLC's brokerage account
  3. The securities move without being sold (no taxable event under IRC § 721)

TAX IMPLICATIONS (IMPORTANT!)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

IRC § 721 NON-RECOGNITION:
  ✓ You do NOT pay tax on the transfer itself
  ✓ No gain or loss is recognized
  ✓ This is like exchanging securities for LLC ownership

CARRYOVER BASIS:
  ✓ LLC takes YOUR original cost basis in the securities
  ✓ Example: If you bought INTC at $12/share, LLC basis is $12
  ✓ Not the $15.58 fair market value at transfer
  ✓ This is CRITICAL for future gain/loss calculations

HOLDING PERIOD:
  ✓ LLC's holding period INCLUDES your holding period
  ✓ Example: If you owned CROX for 2 years, LLC starts with 2-year holding
  ✓ Important for long-term vs short-term capital gains

IRC § 704(c) ALLOCATIONS:
  ✓ Built-in gain/loss at contribution allocated to YOU
  ✓ Example: INTC worth $18,623 but your basis was $13,982
  ✓ That $4,642 built-in gain is yours if LLC sells
  ✓ Prevents shifting unrealized gains to other members
