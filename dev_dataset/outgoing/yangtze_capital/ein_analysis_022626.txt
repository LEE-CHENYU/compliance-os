8. EIN RESPONSIBLE PARTY — LEGAL ANALYSIS
================================================================================

  The "responsible party" on an EIN application (Form SS-4) is:
    "The individual or entity that controls, manages, or directs the
     entity and the disposition of its funds and assets."

  ATTORNEY POSITIONS:
    STEPANOVA (Murthy): "If you are an agent for the company, then that
                        becomes a management decision" — user should NOT
                        be responsible party
    BOX (Mehta):        Did not specifically address EIN responsible party
    ARORA:              Did not address this issue

  ANALYSIS:
    The EIN "responsible party" designation is technically a TAX concept
    (IRS Rev. Proc. 2014-10) identifying who IRS contacts for tax matters.
    It does NOT necessarily equal a corporate officer, director, or
    controlling shareholder.

    HOWEVER, if USCIS sees that the H-1B beneficiary is listed as the
    "responsible party" (i.e., the person who "controls, manages, or
    directs the entity"), this directly contradicts the claim that the
    beneficiary does NOT have controlling interest in the company.

    CONCLUSION: Mother (Meiling Wang) should be the responsible party.
    This is consistent with her role as president and sole director.
