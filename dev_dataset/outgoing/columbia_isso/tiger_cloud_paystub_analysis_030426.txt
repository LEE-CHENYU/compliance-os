9. IMMIGRATION IMPLICATIONS
================================================================================

  A. WAGE LEVEL VS. I-983:
     I-983 states $19/hr. After off-cycle correction, all periods from
     10/01 onward are effectively compensated at $19/hr or above.
     The initial $12/hr paystubs could raise questions if USCIS reviews
     detailed payroll records, but the correction demonstrates the
     employer honored the $19/hr commitment.

  B. HOURS COMPLIANCE:
     The first ~3 weeks of PT (10/01-10/19) show below 20h/week of
     actual work. This is a technical I-983 violation but is mitigated
     by the transition context and the fact that Justworks was still
     processing the payroll changeover.

  C. UNPAID 12/01-12/16:
     These 16 days are the most concerning gap. If USCIS sees SEVIS
     employment through 12/16 but no payroll evidence after 11/30,
     it raises questions about whether employment was real and whether
     the I-983 wage requirement was met during this period.

     RECOMMENDATION: Clarify with Tiger Cloud / Amanda Klier whether
     the actual employment end date was 11/30 or 12/16. If 11/30,
     consider whether SEVIS end date should be corrected.

  D. UNEMPLOYMENT COUNT IMPACT:
     Scenario 1 (employment ended 12/16 as in SEVIS):
       No additional unemployment — Claudius started same day (12/16)
       Cumulative total: ~106 / 150 days

     Scenario 2 (employment effectively ended 11/30):
       15-day unemployment gap: 12/01 – 12/15
       Cumulative total: ~121 / 150 days (29 days remaining)

     Either way, within the 150-day limit, but Scenario 2 is tighter.

  E. MATERIAL CHANGE TIMING:
     I-983 material change was signed 12/09/2024 but effective 10/01/2024.
     This retroactive effective date means the PT arrangement was
     reported ~2 months late. The Columbia ISSO approved it 12/11/2024.
     USCIS could question why the change was not reported at the time
     it occurred, though DSO approval provides some cover.
