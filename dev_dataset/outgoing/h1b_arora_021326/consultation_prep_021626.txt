THE COMPLIANCE CONCERN:
──────────────────────────
  STEM OPT does NOT allow self-employment. Unlike regular OPT, STEM OPT
  requires:
    1. Bona fide employer-employee relationship
    2. Employer enrolled in E-Verify
    3. Signed I-983 training plan
    4. Student may NOT be self-employed

  BSGC was dropped from the SEVIS record when STEM OPT started — only
  Tiger Cloud LLC was listed. But the LLC continued operating (expenses
  on Schedule C, bank activity, capital contributions, trading).

  KEY LEGAL QUESTIONS:
    a. Does passive investment management through an LLC constitute
       "employment" or "self-employment" under 8 CFR 214.2(f)?
    b. The 2024 Schedule C with $69 expenses and the ongoing LLC bank
       activity — is this discoverable by USCIS during H-1B adjudication?
    c. If USCIS cross-references tax returns with SEVIS, they'd see
       Schedule C activity during a period when only Tiger Cloud was the
       authorized STEM OPT employer. What's the risk?
    d. Does this affect the H-1B petition? Or is it only a concern for
       future green card / visa stamp / extension adjudication?
    e. Should we proactively address this, or leave it alone unless asked?
