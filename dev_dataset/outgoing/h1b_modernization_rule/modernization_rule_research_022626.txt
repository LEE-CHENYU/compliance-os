2. OWNER-BENEFICIARY: 100% SELF-SPONSORSHIP IS PERMITTED
================================================================================

  The rule explicitly codifies that a beneficiary who owns MORE THAN 50%
  of (or has majority voting rights in) the petitioning entity may be
  eligible for H-1B status.

  USCIS FAQ (direct quote):
    "A company in which you have a controlling interest — meaning, you
     own more than 50% or have majority voting rights in the company —
     may qualify as your employer and may petition for H-1B status on
     your behalf."

  KEY REQUIREMENTS:
    1. Must be a formal legal entity (LLC, S-Corp, C-Corp)
       → Sole proprietorships are EXCLUDED (no legal separation)
    2. Beneficiary must perform specialty occupation duties MAJORITY of time
    3. Non-specialty duties must be directly related to owning/directing
       the business (signing leases, investor relations, contracts,
       business planning, talent acquisition)
    4. Company needs: bona fide job offer, legal US presence, amenable
       to service of process, IRS Tax ID (EIN)

  NO PARTNER REQUIRED. NO 49/51 SPLIT REQUIRED. NO INDEPENDENT BOARD
  OF DIRECTORS REQUIRED. A 100% single-member LLC qualifies.

3. VALIDITY PERIODS — 18-MONTH vs 3-YEAR
================================================================================

  8 CFR 214.2(h)(9)(iii)(E):

  ┌──────────────────┬───────────────────────┬──────────────────────────┐
  │ Period           │ Controlling (>50%)    │ Non-Controlling (≤50%)   │
  ├──────────────────┼───────────────────────┼──────────────────────────┤
  │ Initial petition │ 18 months max         │ Up to 3 years            │
  │ 1st extension    │ 18 months max         │ Up to 3 years            │
  │ 2nd+ extensions  │ Up to 3 years         │ Up to 3 years            │
  │ Maximum stay     │ 6 years (standard)    │ 6 years (standard)       │
  └──────────────────┴───────────────────────┴──────────────────────────┘

  PRACTICAL EFFECT: Two 18-month periods (3 years total), then standard
  3-year extensions up to the 6-year H-1B max. This is a more frequent
  renewal requirement, NOT a cap on total stay.

4. PRE-2025 vs POST-2025 — WHY STEPANOVA WAS WRONG
================================================================================

  PRE-2025 (NEUFELD MEMO ERA, 2010-2025):
  ─────────────────────────────────────────
    The Neufeld Memo (January 8, 2010) established the "right to control"
    test. When beneficiary = owner, the company couldn't easily demonstrate
    it had the "right to control" the beneficiary's work.

    ATTORNEY WORKAROUND: 49/51 ownership split with a partner. The 51%
    holder (friend, family member) demonstrated "right to control."
    Complex board structures with independent directors were also used.

    NOTE: Neufeld Memo was partially rescinded on June 17, 2020 (ITServe
    Alliance settlement), relaxing the standard to "at least one" of the
    hire/pay/fire/supervise factors. But uncertainty remained until 2025.

  POST-JANUARY 17, 2025 (MODERNIZATION RULE):
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────
    1. REMOVED employer-employee relationship requirement from "US employer"
       definition for beneficiary-owned petitioners
    2. EXPLICITLY CODIFIED that >50% owners are eligible
    3. ELIMINATED the need for independent board structures
    4. DEFINED "controlling interest" clearly in regulations for first time
    5. PERMITTED entrepreneurial duties alongside specialty occupation work

  STEPANOVA'S ADVICE (Feb 26, 2026 consultation):
    → "Self-sponsorship is not allowed for H-1B"
    → "Get a partner, 49/51 split"
    → This is PRE-2025 NEUFELD MEMO advice, 13 months outdated
    → Her own firm (Murthy) published about the Modernization Rule
       on January 27, 2025: "New Regulation Simplifies Process for
       H1B Entrepreneurs" (murthy.com/2025/01/27/...)

5. C-CORP STRATEGY — PACIFIC RIVER CAPITAL ANALYSIS
================================================================================

  STRUCTURE: User owns NO shares. Mother (Meiling Wang) is sole
  shareholder, president, and sole director.

  LEGAL ANALYSIS:
    The 18-month limitation applies when "the beneficiary owns more than
    50% of the petitioner or has majority voting rights." If user owns
    NO shares and has NO voting rights → technically NOT a "beneficiary-
    owner with controlling interest" → 18-month limitation may NOT apply
    → Standard 3-year validity potentially available.

  ATTORNEY POSITIONS:
    BOX (Mehta):      C-Corp with family member = independent, not self-
                      employment. Standard 3-year possible. BUT warned:
                      family-owned invites scrutiny on whether job is real.
    ALCORN:           "Might qualify for 3-year."
    ARORA:            Entity type doesn't matter. Recommended Yangtze
                      initially, then walked it back to "doesn't matter."
    STEPANOVA:        Did NOT analyze this. Recommended 49/51 split instead.
                      Said family relationship "may also be a problem."

  RISKS:
    1. USCIS skepticism of family-owned companies — may look beyond
       formal structure to determine de facto control
    2. The regulation does NOT include "related party attribution" —
       mother's ownership is NOT imputed to child
    3. But USCIS has broad discretion to scrutinize: genuine job, salary
       will be paid, ability to pay, legitimate employer-employee
       relationship
    4. Site visits may probe actual power dynamics

6. $78,000 FILING FEE
================================================================================

  AUTHORITY: Presidential Proclamation, September 19, 2025
             Sections 212(f) and 215(a) of the INA

  DOES NOT APPLY TO:
    ✓ Change of status (F-1 → H-1B) — EXEMPT
    ✓ Extension of stay for persons already in H-1B
    ✓ Amendment petitions for H-1B workers in the US
    ✓ Change of employer for H-1B workers in the US

  APPLIES TO:
    ✗ New H-1B petitions for beneficiaries OUTSIDE the US
    ✗ Petitions requesting CONSULAR PROCESSING (even if beneficiary
      is currently in the US)

  CONFIRMED BY: Box, Alcorn, Murthy (FAQ #10), USCIS alert, Yale OISS

  STRATEGIC IMPLICATION: COS (change of status) is the strongly preferred
  filing strategy. Consular processing would trigger the $78K fee.

7. DUAL REGISTRATION
================================================================================

  CAN MULTIPLE COMPANIES REGISTER THE SAME BENEFICIARY? YES.

  The beneficiary-centric selection process (FY2025 onwards) means each
  beneficiary gets ONE lottery entry regardless of how many registrations
  are submitted. If selected, ALL registering employers are notified.

  DHS initially proposed prohibiting related entities from dual-registering
  (8 CFR 214.2(h)(2)(i)(G)) but DID NOT FINALIZE this provision. Average
  registrations per beneficiary for FY2026: 1.01.

  ATTORNEY POSITIONS:
    BOX:      Both companies can register (correct)
    ALCORN:   "Only one" (likely practical advice — dual reg doesn't
              improve odds, not a legal prohibition)
    ARORA:    Both can register, $663 each (correct)

  FY2027 WEIGHTED SELECTION: Wage-based weighted lottery. If multiple
  registrations, system uses the LOWEST wage level → actually a
  disadvantage. Weights: Level IV = 4x, Level III = 3x, Level II = 2x,
  Level I = 1x.
