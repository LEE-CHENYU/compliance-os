5. QUESTIONS — PRIORITY ORDER
================================================================================

  Open with: "Thank you for taking the time. I sent a detailed email to
  info@ — have you had a chance to review it?"

  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  BLOCK A: ENTITY STRATEGY (10 minutes)
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  A1. "Given my two entities — the Wyoming SMLLC where I'm 100% owner,
      and the California C-Corp where my mother is president and sole
      director — which should file the H-1B petition, and why?"

      GOOD ANSWER: Nuanced analysis of both options. C-Corp may allow
      3-year validity if mother's oversight role is genuine. SMLLC caps
      at 18 months. But USCIS may look through family structures.
      BAD ANSWER: Snap recommendation without reasoning, or "it doesn't
      matter" without explaining the validity period implications.

  A2. "Does USCIS treat a family member (mother) as an independent
      decision-maker, or do they apply de facto control analysis and
      still cap at 18 months?"

      GOOD ANSWER: Cites specific USCIS guidance or case outcomes. May
      say it's a gray area but provides strategic framework.
      BAD ANSWER: "It should be fine" without analysis.

  A3. "Can I only register ONE company for the lottery, or can I
      register through both?"

      Alcorn says: ONE company only. Arora said: can register both
      ($663 each). These conflict — get Box's answer.

  A4. "$78K fee question — am I affected? Does COS vs consular
      processing matter?"

      Only ask this if Box doesn't raise it herself. If she raises it
      unprompted, that's a strong positive signal.

  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  BLOCK B: STEM OPT COMPLIANCE (15 minutes — THE CRITICAL TEST)
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  B1. "During my STEM OPT period (2022-2024), I operated a single-member
      LLC that generated Schedule C income from e-commerce. This was not
      reported to my DSO and was outside my I-983 training plan. Does
      this constitute unauthorized employment under 8 CFR 214.2(f)?"

      GOOD ANSWER: Distinguishes standard OPT (self-employment OK) from
      STEM OPT (employer-employee relationship required). Analyzes whether
      passive e-commerce rises to "employment." Asks follow-up questions
      about the nature of the activity.
      BAD ANSWER: "We don't have an opinion" or "it's fine, don't worry."

  B2. "One employer during that period never signed my I-983. What are
      the implications?"

      GOOD ANSWER: Distinguishes between missing I-983 (procedural) vs
      unauthorized employment (substantive). May cite 150-day unemployment
      tolerance. Discusses whether the gap constitutes a status violation.
      BAD ANSWER: Dismisses without analysis.

  B3. "When filing for H-1B change of status, USCIS requests pay stubs
      and tax returns. My tax returns show Schedule C income from the LLC.
      What is the discoverability risk?"

      GOOD ANSWER: Analyzes the gap between SEVIS records (no LLC) and
      tax records (LLC income). Discusses whether USCIS cross-references.
      Provides realistic risk assessment.
      BAD ANSWER: "USCIS doesn't look at that" without explanation.

  B4. "Is there INA §212(a)(6)(C)(i) misrepresentation exposure?"

      GOOD ANSWER: Analyzes whether anything in the prior record could
      be construed as a willful misrepresentation to gain an immigration
      benefit. Discusses the legal standard.
      BAD ANSWER: Doesn't know what the section is, or dismisses it.

  B5. "Should I affirmatively disclose this in the H-1B petition, or
      remain silent?"

      THIS IS THE MOST IMPORTANT QUESTION. The right attorney should
      have a clear recommendation with reasoning.
      GOOD ANSWER: Weighs pros/cons of disclosure. May recommend
      disclosure with a legal memo explaining the situation. Or may
      recommend silence with specific reasoning about burden of proof.
      BAD ANSWER: No opinion, or "we'll figure it out later."

  B6. "What is the practical risk level — low, medium, or high — for
      the H-1B change-of-status application given this history?"

      GOOD ANSWER: Concrete risk assessment with reasoning. May
      distinguish between risks at different stages (lottery, petition,
      COS adjudication, future green card).
      BAD ANSWER: Vague reassurance without substance.
