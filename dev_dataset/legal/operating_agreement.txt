ARTICLE III - MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

3.1   MEMBER-MANAGED
      The Company is a member-managed limited liability company. The Member
      shall have full, exclusive, and complete authority to manage and control
      the business and affairs of the Company.

3.2   POWERS OF MEMBER
      The Member shall have all powers necessary or convenient to carry out
      the Company's business, including but not limited to:

      (a) Opening and maintaining bank and brokerage accounts;
      (b) Executing contracts and agreements on behalf of the Company;
      (c) Buying, selling, and trading securities and investment instruments;
      (d) Hiring employees, contractors, and professional advisors;
      (e) Filing tax returns and maintaining tax records;
      (f) Making capital contributions and distributions;
      (g) Borrowing money and granting security interests;
      (h) Acquiring and disposing of Company property;
      (i) Taking any action necessary for the Company's business.

3.3   STANDARD OF CARE
      The Member shall manage the Company's affairs in good faith and in a
      manner reasonably believed to be in the best interests of the Company.

3.4   COMPENSATION
      The Member shall not receive any salary or guaranteed payment for
      services rendered to the Company, but may receive distributions as
      provided in Article V.

ARTICLE IV - CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS
================================================================================

4.1   INITIAL CAPITAL CONTRIBUTION
      As of the date of formation, the Member has made or shall make capital
      contributions to the Company as documented in the Company's books and
      records.

4.2   ADDITIONAL CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS
      The Member may make additional capital contributions at any time in
      such amounts and forms (cash, securities, or other property) as the
      Member determines.

4.3   CAPITAL ACCOUNT
      (a) A capital account shall be maintained for the Member in accordance
          with Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-1(b)(2)(iv).

      (b) The Member's capital account shall be:
          - Increased by: (i) cash contributions, (ii) fair market value of
            property contributed, (iii) Company income and gains;
          - Decreased by: (i) cash distributions, (ii) fair market value of
            property distributed, (iii) Company losses and deductions.

      (c) A Master Capital Account Ledger shall be maintained documenting
          all contributions, distributions, and capital account balance.

4.4   SECURITIES TRANSFERS
      When securities or other investment instruments are transferred from
      the Member's personal accounts to the Company's accounts:

      (a) Such transfers shall be treated as capital contributions;
      (b) The contribution amount shall be the fair market value of the
          securities on the transfer date;
      (c) All securities transfers shall be documented with:
          - Date of transfer
          - Description and quantity of securities
          - Market price per unit on transfer date
          - Total fair market value contributed
      (d) Supporting documentation shall include brokerage statements showing
          the position transfer or "journaled shares" transaction.

4.5   CAPITAL CONTRIBUTION DOCUMENTATION
      (a) All capital contributions shall be documented in writing via a
          Capital Contribution Consent executed by the Member.

      (b) Each Capital Contribution Consent shall include:
          - Date of contribution
          - Amount or description of property contributed
          - Fair market value (if non-cash)
          - Source of contribution (bank account, brokerage account, etc.)
          - Updated capital account balance

      (c) Capital Contribution Consents shall be maintained with the
          Company's records and organized by calendar quarter.

4.6   NO OBLIGATION TO CONTRIBUTE
      The Member shall have no obligation to make additional capital
      contributions except as voluntarily determined by the Member.

4.7   NO INTEREST ON CAPITAL
      No interest shall be paid on any capital contribution.

4.8   RETURN OF CONTRIBUTIONS
      Capital contributions may be returned to the Member only through
      distributions as provided in Article V, or upon dissolution as
      provided in Article VIII.

ARTICLE V - DISTRIBUTIONS
================================================================================

5.1   DISTRIBUTIONS
      The Member may cause the Company to make distributions at such times
      and in such amounts as the Member determines in the Member's sole
      discretion.

5.2   SOLVENCY REQUIREMENT
      No distribution shall be made if, after giving effect to the
      distribution:

      (a) The Company would not be able to pay its debts as they become due
          in the ordinary course of business (Wyoming Statute § 17-29-405(a));
          or

      (b) The Company's total assets would be less than the sum of its total
          liabilities (Wyoming Statute § 17-29-405(b)).

5.3   DISTRIBUTION AUTHORIZATION
      (a) All distributions shall be authorized in writing via a Distribution
          Authorization executed by the Member.

      (b) Each Distribution Authorization shall include:
          - Date of distribution
          - Amount of distribution
          - Method of distribution (cash, securities, or other property)
          - Destination account (if applicable)
          - Certification that the distribution complies with the solvency
            requirements of Section 5.2
          - Updated capital account balance

      (c) Distribution Authorizations shall be maintained with the Company's
          records and organized by calendar quarter.

5.4   FORM OF DISTRIBUTIONS
      Distributions may be made in:
      (a) Cash;
      (b) Securities or other investment instruments;
      (c) Other property; or
      (d) Any combination of the foregoing.

5.5   SECURITIES DISTRIBUTIONS
      When securities or other investment instruments are distributed from
      the Company's accounts to the Member's personal accounts:

      (a) The distribution amount shall be the fair market value of the
          securities on the distribution date;
      (b) All securities distributions shall be documented with:
          - Date of distribution
          - Description and quantity of securities
          - Market price per unit on distribution date
          - Total fair market value distributed
      (c) Supporting documentation shall include brokerage statements showing
          the position transfer or "journaled shares" transaction.

5.6   TAX DISTRIBUTIONS
      The Member may authorize distributions in amounts sufficient to pay
      estimated federal and state income taxes attributable to Company income
      allocated to the Member.

5.7   TIMING OF DISTRIBUTIONS
      Unless otherwise specified in the Distribution Authorization,
      distributions shall be paid within thirty (30) days of authorization.

ARTICLE VIII - DISSOLUTION AND WINDING UP
================================================================================

8.1   EVENTS OF DISSOLUTION
      The Company shall dissolve upon the earliest to occur of:

      (a) The written consent of the Member to dissolve the Company;
      (b) The death or incapacity of the Member, unless continued pursuant
          to the Member's estate plan;
      (c) The entry of a decree of judicial dissolution under Wyoming law;
      (d) Any other event requiring dissolution under Wyoming law.

8.2   WINDING UP
      Upon dissolution, the Company shall wind up its affairs as follows:

      (a) The Member (or a person designated by the Member) shall serve as
          liquidating trustee;

      (b) The liquidating trustee shall:
          (i)   Pay or provide for all debts and obligations of the Company;
          (ii)  Set aside reserves for contingent or unforeseen liabilities;
          (iii) Sell Company assets or distribute assets in kind to the Member;
          (iv)  Distribute remaining assets to the Member;
          (v)   File a certificate of dissolution with the Wyoming Secretary
                of State.

      (c) The liquidating trustee shall have all powers necessary to wind up
          and liquidate the Company's business and affairs.

8.3   DISTRIBUTION ON DISSOLUTION
      After payment of all debts, obligations, and expenses, and after setting
      aside reserves, the remaining assets shall be distributed to the Member
      in accordance with the Member's positive capital account balance.

8.4   NO LIABILITY FOR NEGATIVE CAPITAL ACCOUNT
      The Member shall not be obligated to restore any negative capital
      account balance.

ARTICLE IX - LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION
================================================================================

9.1   LIMITED LIABILITY OF MEMBER
      (a) The Member's liability for the Company's debts, obligations, and
          liabilities shall be limited to the Member's capital contributions
          to the Company.

      (b) The Member shall not be personally liable for any debt, obligation,
          or liability of the Company, whether arising in contract, tort, or
          otherwise, solely by reason of being a member of the Company.

      (c) The assets of the Company shall be the sole recourse for the
          Company's debts, obligations, and liabilities.

9.2   NO PERSONAL GUARANTEES IMPLIED
      This Agreement does not create any personal guarantee or personal
      liability of the Member for Company debts or obligations, except to
      the extent the Member voluntarily executes a personal guarantee in
      writing.

9.3   INDEMNIFICATION
      (a) The Company shall indemnify the Member against any losses, damages,
          liabilities, expenses, and costs (including reasonable attorneys'
          fees) arising from any claim related to the Company's business,
          except to the extent such losses result from the Member's:
          (i)   Willful misconduct;
          (ii)  Knowing violation of law;
          (iii) Breach of this Agreement;
          (iv)  Improper personal benefit.

      (b) Indemnification shall be paid from Company assets and shall not
          be a personal obligation of the Member.

9.4   INSURANCE
      The Company may purchase and maintain insurance on behalf of the Member
      against any liability asserted against or incurred by the Member in
      connection with the Company's business.

9.5   CHARGING ORDER PROTECTION
      (a) Under Wyoming law, if a judgment creditor of the Member obtains a
          charging order against the Member's membership interest, such
          creditor shall only have the rights of an assignee and shall not
          become a member or have any management rights.

      (b) The creditor's sole remedy shall be to receive distributions, if
          and when made by the Company to the Member.
