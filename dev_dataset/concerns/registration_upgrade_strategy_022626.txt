6. SWITCHING ATTORNEYS BETWEEN PHASES — MECHANICS
================================================================================

  The registration and petition are TWO SEPARATE USCIS processes:
    1. Registration: Employer submits basic info via myUSCIS portal
    2. Petition: Employer files Form I-129 with full documentation

  There is NO legal or technical barrier to using different attorneys
  for each phase. The registration attorney submits the electronic
  registration; the petition attorney prepares and files the I-129.

  The LCA (Labor Condition Application) is filed AFTER selection but
  BEFORE petition. The petition attorney handles the LCA. This is
  a clean handoff point.

  WHAT THE REGISTRATION ATTORNEY NEEDS:
    - Employer info (name, address, EIN, NAICS code)
    - Beneficiary info (name, DOB, passport, country of birth)
    - Job info (title, SOC code, work location, wage level)
    - Passport bio page copy

  WHAT THE PETITION ATTORNEY NEEDS (only if selected):
    - Everything above, PLUS:
    - Specialty occupation analysis
    - Beneficiary qualifications (degree, transcripts, credentials eval)
    - Employer ability to pay (tax returns, bank statements)
    - Job description, duties breakdown
    - STEM OPT compliance analysis/disclosure strategy
    - Entity analysis (C-Corp vs LLC, 18-mo vs 3-yr)
    - Supporting evidence
