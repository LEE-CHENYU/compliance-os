5. RED FLAGS ANALYSIS
================================================================================

  RED FLAG #1: CONSULTING COMPANY vs. LAW FIRM (HIGH CONCERN)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    ISSUE: Hibee International Consulting Inc. is explicitly NOT a law firm.
    Its website states: "Hibee does not itself provide legal advice." Yet the
    company markets immigration services including H-1B, O-1, L-1, and green
    card applications — services that require legal representation.

    STRUCTURE: The legal work appears to be channeled through Sun & Ren PLLC,
    with Wenjie Sun serving in a dual capacity (Founding Partner of Sun & Ren
    PLLC and Of Counsel to Hibee). This is a LEGAL GRAY AREA:

    - If Hibee functions purely as a marketing/consulting front-end that
      refers clients to Sun & Ren PLLC for legal work, this is potentially
      acceptable but raises questions about fee-splitting with non-attorneys
      (prohibited under NY ethics rules) and unauthorized practice of law.

    - NY Rule of Professional Conduct 5.4(a) prohibits lawyers from sharing
      legal fees with nonlawyers, with limited exceptions. If Hibee (owned
      by non-attorney Chuyi Wang) receives a portion of legal fees, this
      could be an ethics violation.

    - NY Rule 5.4(b) prohibits forming a partnership with a nonlawyer if
      any of the activities of the partnership consist of the practice of law.

    - The key question is: Does the client's retainer agreement name SUN &
      REN PLLC as the legal services provider, or HIBEE? If the engagement
      letter is with Sun & Ren PLLC and Hibee merely makes the introduction,
      this may be acceptable. If the engagement letter is with Hibee, this
      is a potential unauthorized practice of law issue.

    COMPARISON: This is similar to how some Chinese-owned consulting companies
    in the US market immigration services to Chinese clients but use licensed
    attorneys for the actual legal work. Some of these arrangements are
    legitimate; others have resulted in ethics complaints and fraud charges.

    SEVERITY: MEDIUM-HIGH — Not inherently disqualifying, but requires
    clarification. Before engaging, ASK:
      1. "Will my retainer agreement be with Sun & Ren PLLC or with Hibee?"
