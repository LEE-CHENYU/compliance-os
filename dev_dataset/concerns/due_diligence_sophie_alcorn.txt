7. "100% H-1B APPROVAL RATE" CLAIM
================================================================================
  Sources:
    - Website (alcorn.law/h-1b-and-other-h-visas/): Claims "95% approval
      rating for our clients"
    - LinkedIn (Alcorn Immigration Law company page): Claims "100% approval
      rate" in a specific year when the national average was 73%

  ANALYSIS:
    - There is a DISCREPANCY between the 95% website claim and the 100%
      LinkedIn claim.
    - The 95% figure on the website appears to be a career-wide metric.
    - The 100% figure on LinkedIn appears to be year-specific.
    - NEITHER figure is independently verifiable. USCIS does not publish
      approval/denial rates by individual attorney or firm.
    - The DOL OFLC LCA disclosure data (which IS public) only shows Labor
      Condition Application filings, NOT USCIS petition outcomes.
    - No third-party database (MyVisaJobs, H1BGrader, etc.) has data
      on Alcorn Immigration Law's petition outcomes.

  IMPORTANT CONTEXT:
    - USCIS H-1B approval rates for well-prepared petitions from
      experienced attorneys typically range 85-95%+. A 95% claim is
      within the plausible range for a quality immigration practice.
    - A 100% rate in a single year is also plausible for a smaller firm
      that carefully screens cases and only files strong petitions.
    - "Approval rate" can be defined in multiple ways: initial approvals
      only, approvals after RFE, overall outcomes including withdrawals, etc.
    - No immigration attorney's approval rate is independently auditable.

  VERDICT: The 95% career claim is plausible for a quality practice.
  The 100% year-specific claim is possible for a selective firm in a
  single year. NEITHER can be independently verified. The discrepancy
  between 95% (website) and 100% (LinkedIn) suggests the firm is using
  different measurement periods, which is common but slightly misleading.
