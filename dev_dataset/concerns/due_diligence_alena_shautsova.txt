6. THE "89 SELF-SPONSORED H-1B APPROVALS IN Q1 2025" CLAIM
================================================================================

  THE CLAIM: "We've secured 89 self-sponsored H-1B approvals in Q1 2025 alone
  for clients in AI, cleantech, and advanced manufacturing."

  SOURCE: The claim appears ONLY on shautsova.com, specifically in:
    https://shautsova.com/2025/06/11/self-sponsoring-your-h-1b-visa-a-
    comprehensive-guide-for-entrepreneurs-in-2025/
  Published: June 11, 2025

  INDEPENDENT VERIFICATION: NONE FOUND
    - The claim is not cited by any third-party source
    - No independent media or legal publication has verified this number
    - The USCIS H-1B Employer Data Hub does not list individual attorney firms
    - H1BGrader and MyVisaJobs would list petitioning employers, not attorneys
    - The only source for this number is Shautsova's own marketing material

  PLAUSIBILITY ASSESSMENT:
    89 self-sponsored H-1B approvals in a single quarter is a VERY HIGH number.
    For context:
      - Self-sponsored H-1B became explicitly permitted on January 17, 2025
      - Q1 2025 = January-March 2025 (only 2.5 months after rule took effect)
      - This would mean ~30 approvals per month, or ~7 per week
      - Premium processing takes 15 business days; regular can take 6+ months
      - For 89 approvals in Q1, most would need to have been filed very rapidly
        after the Jan 17 rule AND used premium processing
      - This is possible for a high-volume firm, but exceptionally unusual for
        a firm with 7 total staff members (1 attorney, 1 senior paralegal,
        1 legal assistant, 2 other team members, 1 legal secretary, 1 marketing)

  ADDITIONAL UNSOURCED STATISTICS IN THE GUIDE:
    - "63% of RFEs in Q1 2025 citing insufficient board governance documents"
    - "41% denials" for pre-revenue startups without binding contracts
    - "22% of denials linked to incorrect OES wage level selections"
    - Neither statistic is sourced or independently verifiable
    - USCIS does not publish RFE breakdown data at this granularity

  VERDICT ON THE CLAIM:
    UNVERIFIED — TREAT WITH SKEPTICISM
    The claim is plausible but unsubstantiated. During consultation, ask for:
      (1) Case list or anonymized approval notices demonstrating volume
      (2) Specific examples of self-sponsored H-1B cases handled
      (3) RFE rate for her self-sponsored cases specifically
      (4) Whether "89 approvals" means initial approvals, change-of-status,
          extensions, or a combination
