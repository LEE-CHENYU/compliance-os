## Why LinkedIn is Guardian's Primary Channel

The self-directed legal/tax navigator (founders sponsoring own visa, international students moving OPT→H-1B, early employees at small companies) lives on LinkedIn — not on TikTok, not on Reddit. They use LinkedIn for the same career-paperwork concerns Guardian addresses, and they trust people more than brands on LinkedIn.

- **Cheney's personal account** (913 connections) carries trust no paid acquisition can replicate
- **Founder-built immigrant tools** punch above brand weight on LinkedIn — the audience defaults to people, not products
- **Bilingual EN/中文 distribution** has zero competition on LinkedIn — most immigrant-focused tools post English-only

Posting split: **80% personal, 20% company page.** Empty company pages with founder-voice posts feel hollow; loaded personal accounts with consistent founder narrative compound.

## 2. Audience Segments

Each post should target ONE of these segments. Generic content gets scrolled past.

### A. Immigrant founders (YC + adjacent) — highest LTV
- **Signal:** "Founder / Co-Founder / CEO" + visible immigrant background (international university, name pattern, prior post mentions visa)
- **Pain points:** Sponsoring own visa (O-1, EB-1, H-1B via own company); board resolutions that conflict with petition; 1099 income that complicates ability-to-pay
- **Message angle:** "Founder-founder peer talk. Cross-domain edge cases. Specific filing examples."

### B. International students moving OPT → STEM OPT → H-1B
- **Signal:** PhD/Master's at US university, currently on F-1, posts about US-job search
- **Pain points:** I-983 / offer-letter mismatches, EAD renewal timing, H-1B selection
- **Message angle:** "The mistakes that catch students unaware. Free Form 8843 generator as wedge."

### C. Early employees at small companies (no retained legal)
- **Signal:** Engineer/PM at <100-person company, recent F-1 or recent EAD
- **Pain points:** Employer-attorney coverage gaps, dual-status tax confusion, employer-not-responding scenarios
- **Message angle:** "When your company's lawyer is moving too slow / doesn't return calls. Self-defense filing checks."

### D. Bilingual Chinese-speaking professionals (Chinese-first niche)
- **Signal:** Chinese name + bilingual posting + US-based career
- **Pain points:** All of the above + dual-language form filling, 中文 instructions, Chinese-licensed attorneys
- **Message angle:** Post in Chinese for this segment. WeChat / Xiaohongshu distribution downstream.

## 4. Content Pillars

Cycle through these 4 pillars. Don't introduce new pillars without retiring old ones — repetition compounds.

| Pillar | Audience | Format | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **P1: Cross-domain case studies** — anonymized RFE stories where immigration ↔ tax ↔ corporate docs conflicted | Founders, early employees | Carousel or short essay | Every Mon |
| **P2: Tool demos / new features** — screenshots, screen recordings, before/after | All segments | Short post + screenshot | Every Wed |
| **P3: Founder arc / pivots** — your story (HappyHunting → Guardian), pivots honestly explained | Founders specifically | Personal narrative | Every other Fri |
| **P4: Free wedge offers** — Form 8843, free first cross-check | International students, first-time founders | CTA-heavy short post | Every Sun |

**Audience-specific content rule:** Tag each post with which pillar/audience. If a post serves 3 audiences, it's probably a generic post and gets scrolled past.

## 6. Founder Story Arc (The Through-Line)

Repeat this narrative arc across posts. People remember arcs, not job titles.

> "Two years ago, six words on my I-983 almost cost me my STEM OPT. My training plan said 'machine learning pipelines.' My offer letter said 'backend infrastructure.' Same job, different language. To USCIS — two different jobs. My lawyer caught it the night before filing.
>
> Most self-directed filers don't have a lawyer reading every line. So I built one.
>
> Guardian cross-checks your immigration, tax, and corporate documents in a single pass. The kind of inconsistency that took my lawyer an hour to catch, Guardian catches in 30 seconds.
>
> We launched May 12 2026 on Product Hunt. The Form 8843 generator is the wedge — free for international students, no signup, takes 90 seconds."

Reuse pieces of this every 2-3 weeks. Vary the angle (sometimes the visa story, sometimes the tool demo, sometimes the bilingual angle), but the spine stays constant.

## 11. Anti-Patterns (Guardian-specific)

- **"Excited to announce we launched on PH!"** — no. Lead with the insight from the launch, not the excitement.
- **Cross-posting Guardian to the Yangtze Capital company page** — entity entanglement risk per the H-1B file. Keep them visually and topically separate.
- **Tagging YC partners or famous founders for engagement** — they'll mute you. Tag only people directly in your story.
- **Posting only link-outs to your blog / Product Hunt** — value must be on-platform. The reader should get the insight without leaving LinkedIn.
- **Posting in English-only** — you're sacrificing the bilingual moat. At least 1 in 4 posts in Chinese for the Chinese-first niche.
- **Generic content** ("Here are 5 immigration tips!") — every post must target one segment. Lists for everyone serve no one.
- **Naming the Yangtze entity in Guardian posts** — don't reference the H-1B sponsor publicly through Guardian's channels. Cleaner narrative.
- **Posting before the lawyer-flagged concern is resolved** — see open item below.

## 12. Open Items (Holding Pattern)

1. **Lawyer-flagged Guardian launch concern** — specifics deferred from counsel. **Hold any binding GTM action that's hard to reverse** (paid ads, public commitments, naming partners) until specifics arrive. Personal posting + DM outreach is reversible — fine to proceed.
2. **Form 8843 wedge live-status** — confirm `guardiancompliance.com/8843` resolves and works before any post links there.
3. **PH launch dependency** — most of Week 1 calendar above hinges on the May 12 launch happening. If launch is delayed, shift all dates by the delta.
4. **Yangtze Capital posts on Cheney's profile** — Yangtze announcements (page launch, ambassador hire) live on Yangtze's company page, not Cheney's personal feed. Personal feed = Guardian-first.
