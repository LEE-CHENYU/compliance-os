# Guardian — 60-Second Demo Video Script

**Tool:** Screen Studio (Mac, $89 one-time) — auto-zoom on click, smooth cursor.
**Format:** 1920×1080, MP4 export. PH accepts up to 60 seconds inline; longer videos must be YouTube-linked.
**Voiceover:** Cheney (founder voice). Light, conversational, slightly accented English is fine — humanizes the brand. Record into Apple Voice Memos or QuickTime; sync over screen recording in Screen Studio.
**Music:** Optional bed at -24dB. Recommend Epidemic Sound "Productive" or "Glassy" categories. No music if voice is clear.

## Director's Notes

### What to do
- **Record voiceover FIRST**, then cut screen recording to match. This flips the usual workflow and produces tighter videos — no awkward gaps where the voice catches up to the visual.
- **Use real documents, not fakes.** Use a redacted version of your own I-983 / offer letter. Authentic > stock. Blur PII with Screen Studio's built-in blur tool.
- **Click latency matters.** If the live demo takes more than 4 seconds to process, cut to a "processing..." card and speed-ramp 2× through the boring middle.
- **End on a single CTA.** Don't pile up "Try it free, follow on X, join Discord, share with friends." One ask: visit useguardian.ai.

### What NOT to do
- ❌ No "What if I told you..." opener. Cliché on PH.
- ❌ No talking-head segments. Adds production overhead and most viewers mute videos. Voiceover only.
- ❌ No fake user testimonials. Only real quotes, only with permission.
- ❌ No music louder than -18dB under voice. PH viewers often watch on speakers.
- ❌ No "we" if you're a solo founder. "I built this" is more honest and more compelling.

### Alt 30-second cut

If you want a shorter version for X / LinkedIn (PH allows 60s; social platforms truncate):

| Time | What |
|---|---|
| 0:00–0:05 | Cold open + "almost cost me my visa" hook |
| 0:05–0:15 | One demo: drop two docs, see one conflict |
| 0:15–0:25 | Form Filler: drop blank, watch fill |
| 0:25–0:30 | Logo + URL + "Free check today" |

Cut from the same master in Screen Studio — don't re-record.
